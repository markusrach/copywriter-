The 1995 original was shot on film with real trucks, real snow, real people. It cost a fraction of what the AI version required — and it became one of the most iconic Christmas ads ever made. The tagline during the AI campaign? "Real Magic."

The lesson isn't that AI can't create. It's that audiences can feel the absence of human stakes. The original had a crew that stood in the cold. A director who fought about the angle of the lights. An editor who agonized over the timing of the music cue. None of that shows on screen — but all of it shows in the result.

Discussion Questions 1. Watch both versions. What specific moments feel different — and why?
2. Coca-Cola's research said consumers rated the AI ad positively. The internet disagreed. Who's right?
3. If you were Coca-Cola's CMO, would you try AI again for 2026? Under what conditions?
4. How does the tagline "Real Magic" interact with the audience knowing the ad is AI-generated?

The Takeaway for Your Career

AI will write first drafts faster than you ever could. That's not your competition. Your competitive advantage is the ability to diagnose why copy fails, apply frameworks like the 7 Laws to fix it, and inject the specificity and emotional truth that no model can automate. The copywriter's job isn't disappearing — it's evolving from writer to editor, strategist, and quality controller.
